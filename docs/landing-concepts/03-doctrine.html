§ 01 — The category problem

The CRM stores what was typed. An operating layer understands what was heard.

Every enterprise revenue team in the world spends its days inside a piece of software designed in 1999 to be a database for sales managers — and treats the gap between that database and reality as a fact of life.

The forecast call is the cleanest demonstration. Leadership sits down on a Friday morning and asks the same question about a handful of deals: is this real? The CRM says 75%, commit, advancing. The rep says the champion is engaged. The forecast category says yes.

Meanwhile the actual signals — the CFO hasn't joined a call in six weeks, the champion's airtime collapsed from 40% to 8%, procurement entered late, a competitor was introduced during pricing — sit fragmented across calls, Slack, email, CRM activity, and stakeholder behavior. The data exists. It is not synthesized. It does not reach the forecast call.

By the time leadership realizes the deal isn't real, the quarter is already gone.

The conventional response to this gap is more dashboards. More scoring. More AI features bolted onto a system whose job was never to interpret behavior in the first place. We think this is a category error.

The CRM is a database for sales managers. Mallin is the operating environment a deal lives inside.
— Working doctrine, March 2026
§ 02 — What we actually built

Not features. A cognitive loop.

Mallin reads every artifact on a deal — calls, emails, Slack threads, calendar invites, CRM activity — and reconstructs the operational state from what's already there. No new logging. No new workflow. The substrate the rep produced by doing the deal is the substrate Mallin runs on.

From that substrate, the same five-element sequence renders on every surface — pre-call brief, Slack alert, manager DM, retrospective, recap email, exec summary:

/ 01

A clear risk

Named in plain operator language. Never a score in isolation. Never "low confidence" without saying what's actually shaky.

/ 02

A prescriptive move

One next step the rep should take. Not a list of considerations. Not three options. The recommendation, with the reasoning underneath it.

/ 03

Quoted evidence

The actual sentence from the actual call, at the actual timestamp. Paraphrased evidence is a trust failure.

/ 04

Temporal proof

When the artifact was generated, what changed since the last reading, how the signal moved across the cycle. Time is the most under-used dimension in sales software.

/ 05

An accountable decision

Approve, modify, reject. Every claim is reviewable. Every action has an approver. Nothing reaches Salesforce or the customer without a human in the loop.

This sequence is non-negotiable. It is the difference between intelligent dashboard and operating environment. If a surface renders only three of the five, it is not a Mallin surface.

§ 03 — The trust contract

Earned reliance scales. Assumed reliance fails under load.

Half the agentic AI shipping in 2026 is irresponsible — blind CRM auto-writes, browser agents that hallucinate, "let me handle your email" pitches that lose customers their accounts. Sales leaders are correctly suspicious.

We are the opposite. Mallin is agentic where it should be and human where it must be — and the distinction is encoded in the product, not promised in the marketing.

Three stages of trust progression

Every CRM field passes through three stages before anything writes itself.

Stage
Behavior
/ STAGE 1
Suggest
Agent shows its read. Looks right · Looks wrong · Edit. No write to Salesforce.
/ STAGE 2
Apply
One-click approve — earned only after confirm-rate data proves the suggestions are reliable on this field, for this customer.
/ STAGE 3
Auto + Confirm
Agent writes first, surfaces Confirm/Undo — unlocked only after Stage 2 hesitation data clears. No skipping stages, no per-customer overrides.

Never-auto fields

Stage. Amount. Close Date. Forecast Category. Even with full permission. Even after trust is earned. Even when statistically safe. The agent never writes these. The human owns revenue-critical state, because the cost of getting these wrong is a missed quarter — not a deleted record.

This is encoded at the type-shape of the product, not as a setting. There is no per-tenant configuration to flip. There is no edge case where the agent writes Stage. The constraint is structural.

The manager is in the thread

High-severity alerts post into the deal Slack thread the manager is already in — gap named in plain language, verbatim next question to ask attached. The agent decides what's a gap. The team decides the response. The manager being in the thread means the rep can't quietly skirt the conversation.

The rep is not delegating thinking. They are delegating synthesis labor.
— Operating principle · fifth doctrine
§ 04 — Why this is infrastructure

One substrate. Many governed outputs.

Once a deal's substrate is reconstructed — every call, every email, every Slack thread, every stakeholder behavior across eighteen months — the same substrate produces every surface the team needs.

Internal: the pre-call brief, the manager DM, the verification alert, the CRM update, the action queue, the exec summary, the retrospective. External: the recap email, the MAP, the customer deck, the stakeholder one-pager. One substrate. Many governed outputs. Nothing auto-sends. Everything approved. Provenance on every claim.

That asymmetry is what makes this a category, not a feature. A better prompt against a poor substrate produces a confident lie. A modest prompt against the right substrate produces operator-grade judgment. Substrate quality is the multiplier.

The category we are building toward is straightforward to state and difficult to earn:

A governed AI operating system for revenue execution.
— Product north star

Not RevOps AI. Not a sales coach. Not a copilot. An operating environment a rep lives inside during an active deal cycle — and that a leadership team checks before the forecast call, because what's true about the pipeline is what's actually true, not what was typed.

Using a system like this in five years will feel obvious. Until then, we're building it carefully, with five design partners, on a substrate we trust, with a trust contract we will not break.
