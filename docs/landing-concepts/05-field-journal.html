PRIVATE PRINTING · DESIGN-PARTNER COHORT

The name mallín belongs to a kind of Patagonian wetland that looks like ordinary grassland from above, but is sustained by an underground current flowing slowly beneath it. Pull the grass back and the water is right there, holding everything up. We took the name because it described, with no embellishment, the thing we kept finding inside enterprise sales cycles: the surface activity that gets typed into the CRM is real, but it is not what sustains the deal. The underground flow does.

↘ here
the gap What the CRM stores is what a rep typed. What the deal is actually doing — signer disengagement, champion airtime decay, procurement entered late, a competitor introduced during pricing — sits fragmented across calls, email, Slack, calendar invites, and stakeholder behavior. By the time forecast day arrives, leadership is looking at one signal and the deal is being run on another.

Mallin reads the second one. Every artifact on every deal becomes substrate; the substrate gets interpreted into verification alerts the manager sees in the deal Slack thread before the rep can quietly skirt the conversation. Three primitives, read / interpret / act. Approval gate on every CRM write. Stage, amount, close date, and forecast category are never auto-written.

Five entries from the field.
