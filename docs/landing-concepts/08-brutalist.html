§ 1. What it does

Mallin reads every artifact on a deal — calls, emails, Slack threads, calendar invites, CRM activity — and reconstructs the operational state. No new logging is required. The substrate the rep produces by doing the deal is the substrate Mallin runs on.

From that substrate, three primitives:

  • Read. Every artifact becomes substrate. Calls in particular, because that is where the truth of a deal lives.
  • Interpret. Behavior over activity. Signer disengagement, champion airtime decay, procurement entered late, competitor introduced during pricing. The agent does not score the deal; it names what changed and why.
  • Act. Alerts post into the deal Slack thread the manager is already in, with the gap named in plain language and the verbatim next question to ask. The agent decides what is a gap. The team decides the response.

§ 3. What it refuses to do

This is the part most agentic AI in 2026 gets wrong, and the part we treat as structural.

3.1 Never-auto fields

The agent never writes Stage, Amount, Close Date, or Forecast Category. Not with full permission. Not after trust is earned. Not when statistically safe. The constraint is encoded at the type-shape of the product, not as a setting. There is no per-tenant configuration to flip.

3.2 Three-stage trust progression

Every other CRM field passes through three stages before anything writes itself.

  • Stage 1 — Suggest. Agent shows the read. Looks right · Looks wrong · Edit. No write to Salesforce.
  • Stage 2 — Apply. One-click approve. Earned only after confirm-rate data proves the suggestions are reliable on this field, for this customer.
  • Stage 3 — Auto+Confirm. Agent writes first, surfaces Confirm/Undo. Unlocked only after Stage 2 hesitation data clears.

3.3 Manager loop-in

High-severity alerts post into the deal Slack thread the manager is already in. Not a DM the rep can ignore. The manager being in the thread means the rep cannot quietly skirt the conversation. The human-supervisory-control pattern from socio-technical systems: AI assists, humans remain accountable.

§ 4. Who this is for

VP Sales, CRO, or Head of RevOps at mid-market B2B companies — 50 to 500 employees, $10M to $100M ARR, 5 to 20 AEs, a CRM. Three buying triggers recur:

  1. The miss. A quarter where the team missed the number and could not explain why. Pipeline looked healthy. Forecast said yes. Deals stopped or slipped at the end.
  2. The scale moment. Going from 5 AEs to 15 and unable to hire RevOps, SE managers, deal desk, enablement, and partner coordinators fast enough to keep up.
  3. The memory loss. Deal context evaporates when reps churn, quarters compress, or a brief lull breaks continuity. They want the operational memory layer the CRM does not organically become.
