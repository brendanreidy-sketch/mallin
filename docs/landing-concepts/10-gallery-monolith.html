An operating layer
for revenue.

We are not building software for sales managers. We are building the operational memory layer the CRM does not, on its own, become.

Mallín Patagonia · I
— PLATE I

What the CRM stores is what was typed.
What the deal does is what was heard.

Every quarter, leadership asks the same question about a handful of deals: is this real? The CRM says 75 percent, commit, advancing. The forecast category says yes.

Meanwhile the actual signals — signer disengagement, champion airtime decay, procurement entered late, competitor introduced during pricing — sit fragmented across calls, email, and Slack. The data exists. It does not reach the forecast call.

— PLATE II

Three primitives.
No chatbot.

Mallin reads every artifact on the deal — calls, email, Slack, calendar, CRM activity — and reconstructs the operational state from what is already there. No new logging. No new workflow.

Verification alerts post into the deal Slack thread the manager is already in, with the gap named in plain language and the verbatim next question to ask. The agent decides what is a gap. The team decides the response.

— PLATE III

By the numbers, only.

Five enterprise cycles tested in retrospect. Closed-won. Stalled. Closed-lost. The system reproduces the moments experienced operators identify in hindsight — but on the call they happened, not in retrospect.

V

Cycles tested

Real enterprise deals replayed through the system — across the won, the stalled, and the lost.

XII

Alerts on the closed-lost

Twelve verification alerts fired across calls 2–6 of the most recent loss. Three at once on the inflection call.

0

False-positive escalations

Across the retrospective set, zero alerts surfaced behavior the operators reviewing the cycle did not, on reflection, agree mattered.
