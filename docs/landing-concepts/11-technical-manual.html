Mallin · A Field Manual I. The Substrate Problem

— Chapter I

The substrate problem.

Why every existing system that tries to answer the question is this deal real? answers it with the wrong material.

Every quarter, leadership sits down on a Friday morning and asks the same question about a handful of deals: is this real? The CRM says 75 percent, commit, advancing. The rep says the champion is engaged. The forecast category says yes. 1

Definition (mallín, n.). A Patagonian wetland sustained by an underground current. The surface looks like grassland. Pull the grass back: water is right there. Meanwhile the actual signals — the CFO has not joined a call in six weeks, the champion's airtime collapsed from forty percent to eight, procurement entered late, a competitor was introduced during pricing — sit fragmented across calls, Slack, email, calendar invites, and stakeholder behavior. The data exists. It is not synthesized. It does not reach the forecast call.

The conventional response to this gap is more dashboards. More scoring. More AI features bolted onto a system whose job was never to interpret behavior in the first place. We treat this as a category error, and we begin from a different premise: the substrate that matters is not the database, it is the artifacts the deal produced as it was being done.

§ 1.1 — The three primitives

Mallin operates by three primitives, applied in sequence to every deal. They are stated here in the form a future operator will encounter them.

// the three primitives of the operating layer read every artifact on the deal becomes substrate interpret behavior is the signal · activity is the noise act the manager is in the thread before the rep can skirt // approval gate · invariant never_auto = [Stage, Amount, Close_Date, Forecast_Category]2

Each of these primitives carries weight that is not obvious until the system is run against a deal that lost. We treat the closed-lost retrospective as the central empirical instrument of this first edition, and Chapter IV walks through the most recent case at length.

§ 1.2 — What the system refuses to do

Half the agentic AI shipping in 2026 is irresponsible: blind CRM auto-writes, browser agents that hallucinate, "let me handle your email" pitches. Sales leaders are correctly suspicious. Mallin is agentic where it should be and human where it must be. The distinction is encoded in the product, not promised in the marketing.

The constraint on never-auto fields — Stage, Amount, Close Date, Forecast Category — is structural, not configurable. There is no per-tenant override. The cost of getting these wrong is a missed quarter, not a deleted record.
