§ I. The Forecast Is The Report. The Truth Is Somewhere Else.

Every quarter, leadership sits on a Friday morning and asks the same question about a handful of deals: is this real? The CRM says 75 percent, commit, advancing. The forecast category says yes. Meanwhile the actual signals — signer disengagement, champion airtime decay, procurement entered late, competitor introduced during pricing — sit fragmented across calls, Slack, and email. The data exists. It does not reach the forecast call.

§ II. What This Broadside Refuses To Promise.

We are not promising autonomy. Mallin never auto-writes Stage, Amount, Close Date, or Forecast Category — not after trust is earned, not when statistically safe. The constraint is structural, not configurable. Every CRM field passes through three stages of trust progression. Earned reliance scales. Assumed reliance fails under load.

§ III. What Mallin Means.

A mallín is a Patagonian wetland sustained by an underground current. The surface looks like grassland. Pull the grass back, and the water is right there, holding up everything visible. The CRM is the surface. The current is everything the CRM never sees. We took the name because it described, without embellishment, the thing we kept finding inside enterprise sales cycles.
