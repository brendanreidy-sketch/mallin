On Reading This Chart.

A mallín is a Patagonian wetland sustained by an underground current. From above it appears as ordinary grassland; pull back the surface and the water is right there, holding up everything visible. We took the name because it described, with no embellishment, the thing we kept finding inside enterprise sales cycles.

On this chart, the grassland is the CRM — what was typed, what was logged, what the forecast call sees. The blue line is the substrate beneath: calls, email, Slack, calendar invites, stakeholder behavior. The deals are placed where the current actually runs, not where the CRM says they are.

What This System Refuses To Do.

Mallin never auto-writes Stage, Amount, Close Date, or Forecast Category. Not after trust is earned. Not when statistically safe. The constraint is structural, not configurable. Every other field passes through three stages of earned reliance before anything writes itself.

Verification alerts post into the deal Slack thread the manager is already in. The agent decides what is a gap. The team decides the response. The manager being in the thread means the rep cannot quietly skirt the conversation.
