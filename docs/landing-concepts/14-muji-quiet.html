a mallín is a kind of patagonian wetland that looks like ordinary grassland from above, but is sustained by an underground current flowing slowly beneath it. pull the grass back and the water is right there, holding everything up.

we took the name because it described, with no embellishment, the thing we kept finding inside enterprise sales cycles. the surface activity that gets typed into the crm is real, but it is not what sustains the deal. the underground flow does.

i.

read.

every call, every email, every slack thread, every calendar invite on every active deal — taken in as substrate, without asking the rep to log anything new.

ii.

interpret.

behavior is the signal. activity is the noise. signer disengagement, champion airtime decay, procurement entered late, competitor introduced during pricing. what the deal is actually doing — not what was typed.

iii.

act.

verification alerts post into the deal slack thread the manager is already in, gap named in plain language, verbatim next question attached. the agent decides what is a gap. the team decides the response.
