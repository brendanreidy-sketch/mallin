— Institutional memory

Knowledge stays.
Even when the reps don't.

Every call, every email, every objection — and every winning move — gets stored in your firm's database. When a live deal mirrors a past pattern, the relevant precedent surfaces automatically. Your firm's playbook compounds with every cycle.

01 — Winning patterns

What worked last quarter shows up on this quarter.

When a live deal mirrors the shape of a closed-won, Mallin surfaces the move that worked — the objection-handler, the proof-point, the discount structure. Plays don't get re-invented every cycle.

02 — Closed-lost shapes

Recognize the loss before it happens again.

When a new deal starts behaving like a past loss — signer disengagement at the same stage, competitor introduced the same way — you know on call 3, not call 8. The retrospective becomes a live prediction.

03 — Day-one agents

New reps ramp at the speed of your playbook.

From their first call, the agents work alongside them — surfacing what worked on similar deals, suggesting the next move based on your team's prior data points. Veteran-level instincts on day one, not month six.

Your firm's institutional memory. Searchable. Compounding. Yours.
