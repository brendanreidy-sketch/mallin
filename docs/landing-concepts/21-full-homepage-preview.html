— Agentic AI for revenue

After every call,
the agents go to work.

Mallin doesn't just listen. It acts. CRM fields drafted from what was said. Quotes generated from your CPQ based on what was agreed. The next-call deck built from the transcript. Your reps stop doing admin. Your leadership signs off on every output.

01 — Updates your CRM

From transcript to Salesforce — drafted, not auto-written.

Mallin understands what was discussed on the call. MEDDPICC fields, stakeholder roles, next-step commitments — drafted from the transcript, surfaced for approval, written when you click. Stage, amount, close date, and forecast stay never-auto.

02 — Builds quotes from your CPQ

Pricing discussed Tuesday. Quote drafted Tuesday.

When pricing or scope comes up on a call, Mallin pulls from your CPQ — units, terms, discounting guardrails — and drafts the quote inside your system. Your rep reviews. Your rep sends. No spreadsheet, no copy-paste, no waiting on deal desk.

03 — Writes the next-call deck

Every transcript becomes the next call.

After every conversation, Mallin builds the next-call deck from the transcript — where you left off, the specific objections raised, the proof points the prospect now needs to see. Your reps walk in prepared every time, without spending Sunday night in Keynote.

You set the playbook. The agents run the plays.

— Institutional memory

Knowledge stays.
Even when the reps don't.

Every call, every email, every objection — and every winning move — gets stored in your firm's database. When a live deal mirrors a past pattern, the relevant precedent surfaces automatically. Your firm's playbook compounds with every cycle.

01 — Winning patterns

What worked last quarter shows up on this quarter.

When a live deal mirrors the shape of a closed-won, Mallin surfaces the move that worked — the objection-handler, the proof-point, the discount structure. Plays don't get re-invented every cycle.

02 — Closed-lost shapes

Recognize the loss before it happens again.

When a new deal starts behaving like a past loss — signer disengagement at the same stage, competitor introduced the same way — you know on call 3, not call 8. The retrospective becomes a live prediction.

03 — Day-one agents

New reps ramp at the speed of your playbook.

From their first call, the agents work alongside them — surfacing what worked on similar deals, suggesting the next move based on your team's prior data points. Veteran-level instincts on day one, not month six.

Your firm's institutional memory. Searchable. Compounding. Yours.
