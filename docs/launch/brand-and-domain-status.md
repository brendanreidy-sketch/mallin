## Decision input

The plan (page 23) treats Execute.ai as a *working name*, not a final commitment, with this exact step listed under Open decisions: *"Domain availability and trademark search are next steps before public launch."*

Given:
- `.ai` TLD is taken and likely expensive to acquire
- No company-namespace collision yet (window is open)
- Trademark status TBD via manual search

**Three paths forward:**

1. **Buy execute.ai through a broker.** Typical 5-figure ask. Worth it only if Execute.ai is the final name and the brand justifies the cost. Don't do this until the category validates (post-Dimitrie signal + 2–3 design-partner conversations confirming the framing lands).

2. **Pivot to executeai.com or a variant.** `executeai.com` should be checked — couldn't get a definitive WHOIS read. Other variants: `getexecute.ai`, `tryexecute.ai`, `executeapp.ai`, `execute.so`, `execute.app`. Any of these are sub-$100 if available.

3. **Pick a different name entirely.** The plan's positioning is *"replace the operational layer of the revenue organization"* — names that capture that without dictionary-word collision: candidates worth brainstorming once the wedge is validated.

**Recommendation:** Park the brand decision. Trademark check is the cheap step (manual USPTO search, ~30 min). Domain decision waits until either (a) Dimitrie signal validates the category, or (b) first design-partner conversation lands and the framing earns its own sticky name in the customer's mouth. Building under a working name is fine; brand spend before product-market signal is premature.
