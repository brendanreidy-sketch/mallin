# Outreach shortlist — first 5

*Goal: validate that the framing lands. Not recruit design partners yet — that word comes later, after they're leaning in. 5 high-context names is enough; you don't need volume, you need quality of feedback per conversation.*

## Pre-qualification rubric (strict)

Score each candidate on three dimensions before reaching out. Include only if **all three** are strong:

| Signal | Strong (👍) | Disqualifying (👎) |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct access** | You can text or DM them. They'll respond within 48 hrs. | You'd need a warm intro through someone else. |
| **Felt the pain** | They've had a deal die from ambiguity / late surprise / champion loss in the last 12 months. They've talked about it. | They run a clean process or transactional deals where this gap doesn't exist. |
| **Honest feedback** | They'll tell you the framing doesn't land if it doesn't. They won't politely nod. | They're risk-averse / consensus-driven / will give you "interesting" no matter what. |

**Disqualifiers (any one is a no):**
- Highly rigid procurement (slow pilots — burns 60+ days on legal alone)
- Privacy-locked org (healthcare HIPAA / fed gov / financial services with strict data residency)
- Heavy Gong-as-system-of-record investment (their answer to "what's going on with this deal" is already "check Gong" — wedge weakens)

## First two messages, not five

Send only **2 messages** in week one:

- **1 CRO / VP Sales** (Template A in `outreach-templates.md`)
- **1 Head of RevOps** (Template B)

You're testing whether the framing lands at the two roles that buy. Not whether the messaging scales. Two responses is enough signal to refine the third message — if both responses are flat, the framing is wrong, not the volume.

After the first two land, send #3 only after you've adjusted based on what came back.

## What to listen for (calibration reference)

See `calibration.md` for what real signal sounds like vs. what polite interest sounds like. The two often look identical in the moment; the calibration doc is the filter.

## Discovery call structure (30 min)

Goal: **test the wedge, not pitch the product.** They shouldn't see the URL on the first call unless they explicitly ask.

| Min | What |
|---|---|
| 0–5 | Warm-up. Acknowledge the network tie. *"Wanted to pick your brain on something I'm thinking about — not selling, just learning."* |
| 5–20 | The one question: *"When one of your biggest deals is two weeks from close, how do you decide what your AE should do this week to not lose it?"* Then **shut up and listen.** Don't lead. The shape of their answer tells you whether the wedge fits. |
| 20–25 | One follow-up: *"What's the cost when an AE gets that decision wrong?"* — pulls them into the loss frame and reveals economic urgency. |
| 25–30 | If the conversation is hot: *"I've built something that operates exactly in that gap. Want me to walk you through a target on your list and show you what it sees? No data access required, just give me a company name."* That's the credibility-primitive moment — back to the Intelligence-demo recipe (1 prospect, SEC + earnings + news, 1 insight that earns a "how did you see that?"). If the conversation is lukewarm, end with *"this was useful — mind if I come back when there's something more concrete to look at?"* and don't push. |

## What "yes" looks like

A strong call ends with one of:

- *"Send me what you have"* — reactive, low confidence; they're being polite. Soft signal.
- *"When can you show me on a real account?"* — they want to test it. Strong signal.
- *"My VP needs to see this"* — they're already pre-selling. Strongest signal.

Anything else, particularly *"interesting, let me think about it,"* is a no with extra steps. Move on.

## What to log after each call

Per call (3 lines max):

- **Wedge fit:** did their answer to the discovery question reveal the gap, or did they describe a process that already covers it?
- **What landed:** which phrase or framing drew them in? (Use it again.)
- **What didn't:** which phrase or framing got a flat reaction? (Drop it.)

After 3 calls, the pitch will refine itself.
