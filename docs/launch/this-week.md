## Phase shift: precision → discipline

Up to now the variable that mattered most was **precision** — sharpening the framing, the system voice, the read framework. That work is done. The doc set is tight; the product is shipped; the calibration is locked.

From here, the variable that matters most is **discipline** — whether the playbook gets followed exactly when reality starts pushing back.

Reality will push back in specific shapes:

- A response that's warm but vague — your instinct will be to reply with more context. *Don't.* Stick to the one-iteration rule.
- A 3/3 hit that comes in on a busy day — your instinct will be to defer the demo a few days. *Don't.* The 24-hour window is the asymmetry.
- A leaning-in contact who asks "can you do this on my whole pipeline?" — your instinct will be to say yes. *Don't.* Pull back to one deal, one decision moment, one action.
- A first win that feels smaller than you wanted — your instinct will be to wait for a louder one. *Don't.* The quiet validation IS the signal.

The system is good enough. The playbook is tight enough. The only variable left is whether you follow it exactly. No more building. No more refining. No more preparing.

Just: **run the loop, take the signal, act immediately.**
