# Mallin — internal legal templates

**Status:** DRAFTS. None of these documents are lawyer-approved. Take the
content to your attorney before sending to any third party.

## Contents

- [`nda-mutual.md`](./nda-mutual.md) — mutual NDA template for design-partner
  conversations, prospect discussions, vendor evaluations. Drafted as a
  starting point; lawyer review required before first use.
- [`pilot-agreement.md`](./pilot-agreement.md) — design-partner pilot
  agreement template covering scope, term, IP, data handling, fees
  (free during pilot), and conversion. Drafted as a starting point;
  lawyer review required before first use.

## Why these exist as markdown drafts

During design-partner phase, the legal-review cycle for each new
document is the slow path. Having a versioned working draft means:

1. Each conversation can use a consistent template (no per-deal rewrites).
2. Feedback from one design partner's legal team can be folded into the
   template, benefiting every future conversation.
3. The lawyer review can be a one-time engagement against the canonical
   draft, not a per-document engagement.

## Process for shipping a final version

1. Take the draft to a startup lawyer (recommend: Cooley, Fenwick, or
   Stripe Atlas referral network).
2. Get sign-off on scope, terms, liability caps, jurisdiction, IP.
3. Replace `[PLACEHOLDER]` fields with finalized text.
4. Move the finalized PDF to a contracts management system (PandaDoc,
   Ironclad, DocuSign).
5. Mark the markdown draft here as "superseded by [system]" and keep
   for historical reference.
