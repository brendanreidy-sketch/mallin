# The Mallín Operating Principles

*Not marketing copy. This is the document we use to decide: should we build this feature? should we hire this person? should we enter this market? should we take this customer? If the answer doesn't align with what's below, we don't do it.*

## The thesis
Organizations lose their best thinking every day. The knowledge that actually wins — how the best people do it — lives in their heads, leaks out of thin systems, and walks out the door when they leave.

## Our belief
Teams don't have an information problem. They have an execution problem. CRMs store data. AI generates answers. Neither one makes sure an organization executes consistently, keeps what it learns, or gets better over time. Mallín exists to close that gap.

## Mission (today)
Help every revenue team execute with the consistency, confidence, and judgment of its best people.

## Vision (the destination)
Become the operating system for institutional knowledge — where an organization's best thinking becomes how it decides and executes. Sales is where we prove it first; then wherever an organization's best thinking is worth keeping.

## Principles
1. Institutional knowledge compounds.
2. AI does the work; a human stays accountable.
3. Truth over opinion.
4. Execution over activity.
5. Earn trust every day.
6. Simplicity scales.

## What we will never become
- We will never become another CRM.
- We will never act on your behalf without your permission.
- We will never build AI that lacks transparency.
- We will never prioritize more features over better execution.
- We will never sacrifice trust for speed.

## How to use this
Run every significant decision through it:
- **Should we build this feature?** Does it improve execution, or just add surface area?
- **Should we hire this person?** Do they already hold these principles without being told?
- **Should we enter this market?** Is it a place where an organization's best thinking is worth keeping?
- **Should we take this customer?** Can we serve them without crossing a line above?

If a decision doesn't align with the mission, vision, and principles, we don't do it.
